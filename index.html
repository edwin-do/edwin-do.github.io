So fast, so furious

MenloLab

Software Developer Intern

May 2021 - Dec 2021


My first startup experience. We had a small but close development team, and communicated majority of our daily updates through standup. I started my term with a couple light tickets such as adding skeleton loaders, updating outdated packages and adding simple components in Vue.js ...

Afterwards, I quickly moved on to other new features such as pulling and displaying git information for a repository page, creating a file tree interface where users can navigate, add and delete files and folders, and implementing a tool bar used to zoom within the interface.

In the later half of my term, I was tasked to create a Desktop application that can be installed on Windows and Linux machines. I started out by researching the available options and compiled a table that compares the strengths and weaknesses between each option. The team selected a couple potential candidates, and I quickly created a mock-up for each to see its compatability and identify any early problems. After presenting my findings to the team, we decided to move forward with Tauri and I started developing the application. This application needed to communicate with sockets (linux) or named pipes (windows) using interprocess communication to determine the state of certain visual components. For example, if a connection is established, a status icon will be toggled green to reflect its state.

During my testing with this application, I ran into issues arised from a race condition when refreshing the statuses of multiple networks. I was able to solve this concurrency issue by using locks and some help from a senior developer. I definitely learned a lot during this internship and would love to give everyone at MenloLab a shoutout for helping me along the way.

Case Competition?

MARS Apprentice

Season 17 Apprentice - Winner

May 2021 - Nov 2021


MARS Apprenctice is an experiential learning case competition offered to business and management students at McMaster. We worked closely with five different companies to solve five different problems ... that each company was currently facing. 12 students are selected every season to form two teams of 6 that will compete throughout the season. The companies that we had the opportunity of working with in Season 17 were Microsoft, Telus, Canadian Tire, Jan Kelly, and PepsiCo.

MARS Apprentice widened my perspective on how businesses work. Having spent so much of my time being on the engineering side, it was very refreshing to tackle problems from a new point of view. All other members on my team except for myself, came from a business background. During case introductions,the initial approach that I had in mind would be completely different from theirs. After listening to their thoughts, I realized that they had considered other aspects that I had not thought of.

Through this experience, I learned to see a business problem through multiple different lens. I introduced software and tools that we can leverage to my team, to help our strategy to provide more value to my team. My team showed me how to think like a marketer, how to collect data to derive our insights in order to construct our strategy. By helping each other and receiving feedback from the judges and mentors, our team got better and better after each presentation. In the end, we were able to become the winners of Season 17 MARS Apprentice!
